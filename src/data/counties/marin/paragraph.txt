In Marin County, misdemeanors per 1,000 residents were in the low 20s around 2015 and have steadily decreased over time. By the early 2020s, they dropped to the low teens and are expected to fall into single digits after 2028, with only small increases around 2029 and the early 2030s. Misdemeanor rates will likely remain well below their past peak by the 2030s. Overall, both the forecast and its uncertainty band signal a gradual drop in felony rates.
