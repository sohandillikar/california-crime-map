In Monterey County, both misdemeanors and total felonies per 1,000 residents show a clear, long-term downward trend. Misdemeanors start in the low-20s per 1,000 around 2015 and decline into the low-teens by the early 2020s. Felonies begin just under 8 per 1,000 in 2015 and gradually fall into the mid-4 range by the mid-2020s, with the forecast suggesting continued decline toward near-zero levels by about 2033. Overall, Monterey County appears on track for significantly lower crime rates across both misdemeanors and felonies over the coming decade.
