In Napa County, both misdemeanor and felony rates per 1,000 residents have steadily declined over time. Misdemeanor rates dropped from the high 20s in 2015. Forecasts show this downward trend continuing, with rates expected to fall into the low single digits by the early 2030s. Felony rates had a sharper decline after 2022. Projections indicate felony rates will stay well below past levels and could approach near zero by the early 2030s. To conclude, both graphs suggest that misdemeanor and felony rates in Napa County will remain much lower than in the past decade.
